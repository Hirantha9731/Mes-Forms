TEBS Application Overview

Description of Criteria

This is a Application for the TEBS Servicer. Freddie Mac evaluates a Servicer with respect to the criteria set forth in this Application Form. Approval of a Multifamily Servicer for a TEBS transaction is at Freddie Mac's sole discretion. The Servicer criteria is subject to change.

This Application is for Multifamily TEBS Servicer status only. It is not an application for approval as a Single Family Seller/Servicer, Multifamily Program Plus S/S, or Multifamily TAH S/S.

Additionally, Servicers need not be approved by Single Family prior to applying for Multifamily approval. Multifamily approval does not imply or include approval for Single Family. Multifamily does not have delegated authority to grant Single Family approval. Therefore, a Multifamily TEBS Servicer application does not substitute for a Single Family application.

Eligibility

All Seller/Servicers must abide by the requirements provided in Chapter 3 of the Guide. For your reference, below are the current Net Worth, Loan Servicing and Insurance requirements from the Guide.

Financial Condition: Freddie Mac will review each applicant’s financial condition, capital adequacy, asset quality, earnings, and liquidity. The applicant must meet the minimum eligibility requirements of $5 million minimum net worth as defined by generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP) and liquid assets of $500,000.

Servicing Volume: Completion of the Structured Transaction Servicer Form, as applicable, along with other attachments, will help Freddie Mac evaluate your multifamily servicing operation.

Minimum volume of servicing portfolio must be:

$1 billion of mortgages secured by income properties (including $400 million of mortgages secured by multifamily properties)
or
$500 million of mortgages secured by multifamily properties

Corporate Insurance: All applicants are required to have fidelity insurance and mortgagee's errors and omissions (E&O) insurance coverage as described below. Fidelity insurance protects the applicant against loss resulting from dishonesty or fraud committed by officers & employees of the applicant and by other individuals covered by the policy. E&O insurance protects the applicant against loss resulting from errors and omissions by employees.

A mortgage impairment or mortgage interest policy is acceptable as a substitute for an E&O policy provided that the required level of coverage and terms are provided. E&O coverage per mortgage with a per mortgage limitation policy is acceptable if the insurer's limit of insurance or liability per mortgage is no less than the unpaid principal balance of the largest mortgage originated, sold or serviced by your institution, whichever is highest.

Application Package

As part of the Application, please include the following documentation for review.

  1. Resumes:  Each applicant must submit resumes of the following individuals:
    • Principals (stockholders with holdings of 10 percent or more)
    • Managing Executives and Key Personnel, defined as Chief Executive Officer, Chief Operating Officer, Chief Financial Officer, Chief Servicing Officer, Chief Architect/Engineer, Chief Underwriter, Chief Investor Reporting Officer, Corporate Insurance Contact, Head of Servicing, Head of Production and/or the equivalent of each of these positions.
    • Key LIHTC personnel (division/operating unit heads)

    2. Each resume should include an employment history, with titles and a description of the individual’s responsibilities in each functional area (for example, origination) for each position, years of experience in each area, educational background, and related formal industry training. Please also email each individual's date of birth. This information will be used to conduct the background check.

  2. Financial Statements:  Each applicant must submit an audited annual report (financial statement), including a detailed explanation if the audit opinion is other than unqualified, for its most recent fiscal year-end and audited financial statements for the preceding two fiscal years (total of three years) and financial statements from the past 5 quarters. Note: If the most recent audited annual report is more than six months old, a supplemental financial statement (no more than 90 days old) certified by the applicant’s chief financial officer is required. The financial statements submitted should specifically tie the applicant’s financial statements to the parent’s audited financial statements
  3. System and Organization Control Reports:  Provide a copy of the most recent SOC1, SOC2, SAS 70 report, if applicable.
  4. Entity Organizational Chart:  Provide an entity organizational chart showing the applicant's direct and ultimate parents.
  5. Business Strategy:  
    • Provide the applicant's business plan.
    • Provide a description of the products offered.
  6. Business Continuity Plan:  Provide the applicant's Business Continuity Plan.
  7. Organizational Charts:  Provide a detailed organizational chart showing all functional areas and the names and titles of all LIHTC personnel, tenure with company and LIHTC experience.
  8. Policies & Procedures:  Provide written policies and procedures for originations, underwriting, asset management, and other key LIHTC operational departments. Also, please provide any P&Ps associated with construction monitoring, deal selection, and deal approval.
  9. Corporate Insurance:  Provide a copy of the declaration page of the binder or other acceptable written evidence of corporate insurance. The documentation should specify which entity is covered, type of coverage, coverage amounts, loss payee, deductible, and policy duration.
  10. Project History:  Provide project history describing the types of projects, complexity, and tax credit delivery, including project failures, tax credit delays or recaptures and projects on watchlists.
  11. Additional Documentation:  
    • Samples of typical reports and any other pertinent information that will be regularly communicated to Investors regarding housing tax credit investments
    • Current marketing materials for investors
    • Sustainability Analysis
    • Property site visit checklist and report template
    • Current pipeline of active transactions, sorted by geographic location
    • Summary of any General Partner removals in the last three years
    • Watchlist criteria and the latest watchlist report
    • Sample complete investment committee/approval package with the investment committee roster and approval procedures
    • Underwriting manual
