Multifamily Targeted Affordable Housing Seller/Servicer Form
Overview

ONLY those applicants seeking a Targeted Affordable Housing (TAH) Seller/Servicer license need to complete this form.

By completing the information on this form you will facilitate Freddie Mac's review of the applicant's TAH underwriting, production, servicing, construction monitoring, and conversion management operations. The information you provide, unless otherwise specified, should be as of the date you complete and sign this form.

If the applicant is applying to be a Servicer-Only counterparty, then they will only be required to complete the servicing portion of this form.

For the purpose of this form, servicing staff refers to all full-time multifamily collection, foreclosure, real estate owned (REO), and property inspection staff, as well as support staff including payoff, escrow, and vault/note maintenance personnel.

* Delinquency occurs when all or part of the borrower's monthly installment of principal, interest, and where applicable, escrow is unpaid after the due date. A delinquency that remains uncured as of the close of business of the last working day before the next due date is considered a delinquency of one payment or 30 days, regardless of the actual number of calendar days since the initial due date. A delinquency that has been uncured for 30 days or more but less than 60 days by the end of the reporting month must be reported as a one-payment or 30-day delinquency.

For example: a mortgage due on October 1, would be considered 30 days (or one payment) delinquent as of the close of business on October 31. The mortgage would be considered 60 days (two payments) delinquent if unpaid by the close of business on November 30
