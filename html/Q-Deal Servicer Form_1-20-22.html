Q-Deal Application Overview

Description of Criteria

This is a Application for the Q-Deal Servicer. Freddie Mac evaluates a Servicer with respect to the criteria set forth in this Application Form. Approval of a Multifamily Servicer for a Q-Deal transaction is at Freddie Mac's sole discretion. The Servicer criteria is subject to change.

This Application is for Multifamily Q-Deal Servicer status only. It is not an application for approval as a Single Family Seller/Servicer, Multifamily Program Plus S/S, Multifamily SBL S/S, Multifamily TAH S/S or any other Structured Transaction S/S.

Additionally, Servicers need not be approved by Single Family prior to applying for Multifamily approval. Multifamily approval does not imply or include approval for Single Family. Multifamily does not have delegated authority to grant Single Family approval. Therefore, a Multifamily Q-Deal Servicer application does not substitute for a Single Family application.

Eligibility

All Seller/Servicers must abide by the requirements provided in Chapter 3 of the Guide. For your reference, below are the current Net Worth, Loan Servicing and Insurance requirements from the Guide.

Financial Condition: Freddie Mac will review each applicant’s financial condition, capital adequacy, asset quality, earnings, and liquidity. The applicant must meet the minimum eligibility requirements of $5 million minimum net worth as defined by generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP) and liquid assets of $500,000.

Servicing Volume: Completion of the Structured Transaction Servicer Form, as applicable, along with other attachments, will help Freddie Mac evaluate your multifamily servicing operation.

Minimum volume of servicing portfolio must be:

$1 billion of mortgages secured by income properties (including $400 million of mortgages secured by multifamily properties)
or
$500 million of mortgages secured by multifamily properties

Corporate Insurance: All applicants are required to have fidelity insurance and mortgagee's errors and omissions (E&O) insurance coverage as described below. Fidelity insurance protects the applicant against loss resulting from dishonesty or fraud committed by officers & employees of the applicant and by other individuals covered by the policy. E&O insurance protects the applicant against loss resulting from errors and omissions by employees.

A mortgage impairment or mortgage interest policy is acceptable as a substitute for an E&O policy provided that the required level of coverage and terms are provided. E&O coverage per mortgage with a per mortgage limitation policy is acceptable if the insurer's limit of insurance or liability per mortgage is no less than the unpaid principal balance of the largest mortgage originated, sold or serviced by your institution, whichever is highest.

The evidence of insurance should specify the entity covered, type of coverage, coverage amounts, loss payee, deductibles, and policy duration. Examples of calculations of the required minimum insurance limits are as follows:

Reporting Requirements

Annual Reporting: 16M, 17M and 1110M
- Once approved, each SBL, Conventional or TAH Optigo Lender must submit Form 16M Multifamily Annual Certification Report, within 90 days after its fiscal year-end. Form 16M includes a general information statement, and audited financial statements and report on internal control structure.

- Once approved, each Structured Transactions counterparty must submit Form 17M Multifamily Annual Certification Report, within 90 days after its fiscal year-end. Form 17M includes a general information statement, and audited financial statements and report on internal control structure.

- Once approved, each Servicer-only counterparty must submit Form 1110M Multifamily Annual Certification Report annually to Freddie Mac.

Quarterly Reporting: In addition to the annual requirement set forth in the preceding paragraph, each approved institution is required to submit Form 1055, Mortgage Bankers Financial Reporting Form, after the end of each calendar quarter.

* Delinquency occurs when all or part of the borrower's monthly installment of principal, interest, and where applicable, escrow is unpaid after the due date. A delinquency that remains uncured as of the close of business of the last working day before the next due date is considered a delinquency of one payment or 30 days, regardless of the actual number of calendar days since the initial due date. A delinquency that has been uncured for 30 days or more but less than 60 days by the end of the reporting month must be reported as a one-payment or 30-day delinquency.

For example, a mortgage due on October 1, would be considered 30 days (or one payment) delinquent as of the close of business on October 31. The mortgage would be considered 60 days (2 payments) delinquent if unpaid by the close of business on November 30
