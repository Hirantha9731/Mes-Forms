Description of Sponsor and Guarantor Criteria

Freddie Mac requires the deal Sponsor to be organized as a single asset, bankruptcy remote, Special Purpose Entity (SPE). Each SPE/Sponsor will be evaluated with respect to the criteria set forth in this Application Form for Approval. The SPE/Sponsor criteria are subject to change. Since the SPE/Sponsor must be formed for each specific deal and may not exist at the time of approval, Freddie Mac will allow either the Principal Counterparty or the Guarantor to complete and execute this application on the Sponsor's behalf

This Application is for Multifamily TEBS Sponsor and Guarantor status only. It is not an application for approval as a Single Family Seller/Servicer, Multifamily Conventional Seller/Servicer, Multifamily SBL Seller/Servicer or Multifamily TAH Seller/Servicer.

Additionally, the Sponsor and Guarantor do not need to be approved by Single Family prior to applying for Multifamily approval. Multifamily approval does not imply or include approval for Single Family. Multifamily does not have delegated authority to grant Single Family approval. Therefore, a Multifamily TEBS Sponsor application does not substitute for a Single Family application.

Definitions

Definitions of some key terms used by Freddie Mac are provided below.

  1. - first lien mortgage on real estate on which there is located a structure used for office, retail, wholesale, industry and/or storage.
  2. - first lien mortgage on real estate on which there is a structure designed principally for residential use by more than four families.
  3. - bonds issued to provide financing for multifamily housing properties, the interest on which is exempt from federal income taxation
  4. - Freddie Mac's Tax Exempt Bond Securitization program, pursuant to which Freddie Mac acquires and securitizes Tax Exempt Bonds and related taxable bonds.
  5. - the institution that:
    • Has aggregated the Bonds included in the deal submission.
    • Is authorized to sign the Deal Term Sheet
    • May form the Special Purpose Entity (SPE) that will serve the Deal Sponsor , and
    • May complete and execute the Freddie Mac Sponsor/SPE application on behalf on the to-be formed entity.
  6. - a single asset, bankruptcy remote Special Purpose Entity created and approved to sell mortgages and/or bonds to Freddie Mac.
  7. - an institution approved to service mortgages purchased by Freddie Mac and/or bonds purchased or credit enhanced by Freddie Mac.
  8. - a transfer of servicing that occurs simultaneously with the purchase of mortgages and/or bonds by Freddie Mac.
