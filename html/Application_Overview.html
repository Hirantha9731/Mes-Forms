Application Overview
Overview

Welcome to Freddie Mac Multifamily's Counterparty Application process. Freddie Mac evaluates all counterparties that wish to sell or service Conventional, Targeted Affordable Housing (TAH), Small Balance Loans (SBL), or Structured Transaction loans for compliance with our requirements. These requirements are detailed in the Multifamily Seller / Servicer Guide, which is available at https://mf.freddiemac.com/lenders/guide, and are subject to change. A Counterparty application is required for each approval type requested (i.e., Conventional, TAH, SBL, or Structured Transactions).

Approval of an institution is at Freddie Mac’s sole discretion. This Application is for Multifamily Seller/Servicer status only; it is not an application for approval as a Single Family Seller/Servicer. If approval for both Single Family and Multifamily is desired, separate applications must be submitted in accordance with the requirements under each designation. Additionally, applicants need not be approved by Single Family prior to applying for Multifamily approval. Multifamily approval does not imply or include approval for Single Family. Multifamily does not have delegated authority to grant Single Family approval, therefore a Multifamily Seller/Servicer application does not substitute for a Single Family Application.

Key term definitions used by Freddie Mac in this application process are provided below:

  1. Optigo Lender : An entity approved to sell and service Conventional, Targeted Affordable, or Small Balance multifamily mortgages to Freddie Mac. These loans are originated on Freddie Mac paper for the purpose of being sold to Freddie Mac.
  2. Seller / Servicer : A lender that both sells and services loans on behalf of Freddie Mac.
  3. Structured Transaction Seller : Entities approved to sell Structured Transaction multifamily loans to Freddie Mac. These loans are originated off Freddie Mac paper, and these entities are approved on a deal-by-deal basis.
  4. Structured Transaction Servicer : Entities approved to service Structured Transaction multifamily loans to Freddie Mac. These loans are originated off Freddie Mac paper, and these entities are approved on a deal-by-deal basis.
  5. Targeted Affordable Housing (TAH) : Loans for properties in underserved areas that are affordable to families with low and very-low incomes including cash loans, bond credit enhancements, tax-exempt loans, and others.
  6. Small Balance Lending (SBL) : Loans for small apartment buildings meeting the Multifamily Guide definition of Small Balance (as of 2022, targeting 5 to 50 units, $1 million to $7.5 million).
  7. Multifamily Mortgage : First lien mortgage on real estate on which there is a structure designed principally for residential use by five or more families.
  8. Commercial Mortgage : First lien mortgage on real estate on which there is located a structure used for office, retail, wholesale, industrial and/or storage.
Instructions

For consideration of Freddie Mac Multifamily approval, the applicant must complete the entirety of the application. This includes:

  • Submission of all assigned forms
  • Submission of all requested documentation
  • Payment of the application fee

Freddie Mac will not begin to review the application until all of the above is submitted and confirmed to be complete. Once determined to be complete, Freddie Mac will take 60 days to return a decision to the applicant. Freddie Mac will seek to verify any submitted application information as part of the review of the application package. An on-site review of an applicant’s facilities and operations may be necessary as part of the approval process.

While the application is pending action by Freddie Mac, the applicant must notify Freddie Mac in writing of any material factors that could affect the application decision. Each applicant must also certify that, if approved, it will comply with all the terms, conditions, warranties, representations, and requirements set forth in the purchase documents, including the Freddie Mac Multifamily Seller Servicer Guide, governing the sale of mortgages to Freddie Mac and servicing of mortgages for Freddie Mac..

All standards concerning Freddie Mac’s purchase programs and qualifications for Seller/Servicer approval are subject to change by Freddie Mac at any time without notice or publication. The Guide is available on Freddie Mac's Website for reference. If you have any questions, please email: MF_Counterparty_Transactions@freddiemac.com

Eligibility

All Sellers and Servicers must abide by the minimum eligibility requirements provided in Chapter 3 of the Guide for consideration. These include requirements of the applicant's net worth, liquidity, minimum servicing portfolio size, minimum securitization servicing experience, minimum securitization servicing volume, and minimum corporate insurance.

Financial Condition: Freddie Mac will review each applicant’s financial condition, capital adequacy, asset quality, earnings, and liquidity. The applicant must meet the minimum eligibility requirements of $5 million minimum net worth as defined by generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP) and liquid assets of $500,000.

Servicing Volume: Completion of the Conventional, TAH, SBL and/or Structured Transaction Seller/Servicer Forms, as applicable, along with other attachments, will help Freddie Mac evaluate your multifamily servicing operation.

Minimum volume of servicing portfolio must be:
$1 billion of mortgages secured by income properties (including $400 million of mortgages secured by multifamily properties)
or
$500 million of mortgages secured by multifamily properties

Corporate Insurance: All applicants are required to have fidelity insurance and mortgagee's errors and omissions (E&O) insurance coverage as described below. Fidelity insurance protects the applicant against loss resulting from dishonesty or fraud committed by officers & employees of the applicant and by other individuals covered by the policy. E&O insurance protects the applicant against loss resulting from errors and omissions by employees.

A mortgage impairment or mortgage interest policy is acceptable as a substitute for an E&O policy provided that the required level of coverage and terms are provided. E&O coverage per mortgage with a per mortgage limitation policy is acceptable if the insurer's limit of insurance or liability per mortgage is no less than the unpaid principal balance of the largest mortgage originated, sold or serviced by your institution, whichever is highest.

Reporting Requirements

All Freddie Mac Sellers and Servicers must perform ongoing reporting to Freddie Mac if approved. These include:

Annual Reporting: 16M, 17M and 1110M
  • Once approved, each SBL, Conventional or TAH Optigo Lender must submit Form 16M Multifamily Annual Certification Report, within 90 days after its fiscal year-end. Form 16M includes a general information statement, and audited financial statements and report on internal control structure.
  • Once approved, each Structured Transactions counterparty must submit Form 17M Multifamily Annual Certification Report, within 90 days after its fiscal year-end. Form 17M includes a general information statement, and audited financial statements. This form may be waived at Freddie Mac's discretion for Structured Transactions sellers that do not perform servicing.
  • Once approved, each Servicer-only counterparty must submit Form 1110M Multifamily Annual Certification Report annually to Freddie Mac.

Quarterly Reporting: In addition to the annual requirement set forth in the preceding paragraph, each approved institution is required to submit Form 1055, Mortgage Bankers Financial Reporting Form, after the end of each calendar quarter. This requirement is waived if the counterparty has publicly-available financials.

Application Package

As part of the Application, please include the following documentation for review.

  1. Resumes / Bios: Each applicant must submit resumes of the following individuals:
    • Principals (stockholders with holdings of 10 percent or more)
    • Managing Executives and Key Personnel, defined as Chief Executive Officer, Chief Operating Officer, Chief Financial Officer, Chief Servicing Officer (if servicing), Chief Architect/Engineer (if applying for TAH), Chief Underwriter, Chief Investor Reporting Officer (if servicing), Corporate Insurance Contact (if servicing), Credit Risk Officer(s), Head of Servicing, Head of Production and/or the equivalent of each of these positions.
    • Key operating personnel (division/operating unit heads) in the areas of Origination, Underwriting, Loan Servicing, Asset Management, Investor Accounting / Reporting, Default Management (delinquency and foreclosure), and REO servicing,

Each resume or bio should include an employment history, with titles and a description of the individual’s responsibilities in each functional area for each position, years of experience in each area, educational background, and related formal industry training.

  1. Financial Statements: Each applicant must submit an audited annual report (financial statement), including a detailed explanation if the audit opinion is other than unqualified, for its most recent fiscal year-end and audited financial statements for the preceding two fiscal years (total of three years) and financial statements from the past 5 quarters. Please provide quarterly financials in Quarter-to-Date format in Excel. Note: If the most recent audited annual report is more than six months old, a supplemental financial statement (no more than 90 days old) certified by the applicant’s chief financial officer is required. The financial statements submitted should specifically tie the applicant’s financial statements to the parent’s audited financial statements.
  2. Organizational Charts:
    • Provide an entity organizational chart including the applicant's direct and ultimate parents, ownership percentages, and controlling interests.
    • Provide detailed organizational chart(s) showing all mortgage operations personnel, including (as applicable to the requested approval) Production, Underwriting, Asset Management, Loan Servicing, Construction Monitoring and Conversion Management functional areas and the names and titles of all related personnel.
  3. Business Strategy:
    • Provide the applicant's business plan as it relates to the requested Freddie Mac approval.
    • Provide a description of the products the applicant intends to offer to their customers if approved.
  4. Policies & Procedures:
    • Provide all internal written multifamily policies & procedures relevant to the requested approval. These should include underwriting, origination, servicing, and asset management policies and procedures. Freddie Mac Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) will review relevant procedures to assess the adequacy of each applicable area. Please refer to the supplemental Policies and Procedures document provided for a sample list of expected procedures.
    • Provide all corporate written policies & procedures in the areas of information security, privacy, identity and access management, financial crimes, business resiliency, and vendor management.
  5. Sample Documentation: Provide one sample of each of the following:
    • Typical payment history report
    • Escrow analysis
    • Collection letter
    • Inspection reports
    • Borrower KYC due diligence report
    • Construction monitoring report (if applying for TAH approval)
  6. Organizational Control Reports and Test Results: If available, provide the most recent control and testing reports such as:
    • System and Organization Control I or II
    • SAS 70
    • Penetration Testing
    • Controls testing results to prevent, detect and resolve/minimize impact of IT/information security incidents.
    • Other applicable relevant reports or test results.
  7. Business Continuity Plan: Provide the applicant's Business Continuity Plan and any results from resiliency / disaster recovery testing.
  8. Corporate Insurance: Provide a copy of the declaration page of the binder or other acceptable written evidence of corporate insurance. The documentation should specify which entity is covered, type of coverage, coverage amounts, loss payee, deductible, and policy duration.
  9. Warehouse Line: If applicable, provide a copy of the applicant's warehouse line of compliance certification.
  10. Vendor: Please complete and submit the provided vendor inventory
