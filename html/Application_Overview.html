LIHTC Syndicator Application
General Instructions

Freddie Mac evaluates Conventional, Targeted Affordable Housing, Small Balance Loan, and Structured Transaction Seller/Servicer applicants with respect to criteria such as financial condition, management experience, and current and past origination and servicing performance. Approval of an institution is at Freddie Mac’s sole discretion. Freddie Mac’s evaluation criteria are subject to change.

The applicant must have previously submitted the Notification of Interest Form and been approved by Freddie Mac to apply following the Prescreen Process.

This Application is for Multifamily Seller/Servicer status only. It is not an application for approval as a Single Family Seller/Servicer. If approval for both Single Family and Multifamily is desired, separate applications must be submitted in accordance with the requirements under each designation. Additionally, applicants need not be approved by Single Family prior to applying for Multifamily approval. Multifamily approval does not imply or include approval for Single Family. Multifamily does not have delegated authority to grant Single Family approval, therefore a Multifamily Seller/Servicer application does not substitute for a Single Family Application.

Definitions of some key terms used by Freddie Mac are provided below.

  1. Commercial mortgage - First lien mortgage on real estate on which there is located a structure used for office, retail, wholesale, industrial and/or storage
  2. Targeted Affordable - Cash mortgages or tax exempt bond credit enhancements, whose purpose is for financing properties subject to rent and/or income restrictions for the purpose of providing housing to low and moderate income persons or persons renting in under-served areas as defined by HUD for Freddie Mac.
  3. Multifamily mortgage - First lien mortgage on real estate on which there is a structure designed principally for residential use by five or more families.
  4. Servicer - An institution approved to service mortgages purchased by Freddie Mac.
  5. Seller / Servicer - An institution approved to sell mortgages to and service mortgages purchased by Freddie Mac.

Please complete the entirety of the attached application. The entirety of the application package will include:

  • Application Overview

  • Conventional, TAH or SBL Seller/Servicer Forms (if applying for multiple designations, then multiple forms will need to be submitted.)

An on-site review of an applicant’s facilities and operations may be necessary as part of the approval process.

Each applicant must also certify that, if approved, it will comply with all the terms, conditions, warranties, representations, and requirements set forth in the purchase documents, including the Freddie Mac Multifamily Seller Servicer Guide (the "Guide"), governing the sale of mortgages to Freddie Mac and servicing of mortgages for Freddie Mac.

All standards concerning Freddie Mac’s purchase programs and qualifications for Seller/Servicer approval are subject to change by Freddie Mac at any time without notice or publication. The Guide is available on Freddie Mac's Website for reference.

Eligibility

All Seller/Servicers must abide by the requirements provided in Chapter 3 of the Guide. For your reference, below are the current Net Worth, Loan Servicing and Insurance requirements from the Guide.

Financial Condition: Freddie Mac will review each applicant’s financial condition, capital adequacy, asset quality, earnings, and liquidity. The applicant must meet the minimum eligibility requirements of $5 million minimum net worth as defined by generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP) and liquid assets of $500,000.

Servicing Volume: Completion of the Conventional, TAH, SBL and/or Structured Transaction Seller/Servicer Forms, as applicable, along with other attachments, will help Freddie Mac evaluate your multifamily servicing operation.

Minimum volume of servicing portfolio must be:
$1 billion of mortgages secured by income properties (including $400 million of mortgages secured by multifamily properties) or $500 million of mortgages secured by multifamily properties

Corporate Insurance: All applicants are required to have fidelity insurance and mortgagee's errors and omissions (E&O) insurance coverage as described below. Fidelity insurance protects the applicant against loss resulting from dishonesty or fraud committed by officers & employees of the applicant and by other individuals covered by the policy. E&O insurance protects the applicant against loss resulting from errors and omissions by employees.

A mortgage impairment or mortgage interest policy is acceptable as a substitute for an E&O policy provided that the required level of coverage and terms are provided. E&O coverage per mortgage with a per mortgage limitation policy is acceptable if the insurer's limit of insurance or liability per mortgage is no less than the unpaid principal balance of the largest mortgage originated, sold or serviced by your institution, whichever is highest.

Reporting Requirements

Annual Reporting: 16M, 17M and 1110M
- Once approved, each SBL, Conventional or TAH Optigo Lender must submit Form 16M Multifamily Annual Certification Report, within 90 days after its fiscal year-end. Form 16M includes a general information statement, and audited financial statements and report on internal control structure.
- Once approved, each Structured Transactions counterparty must submit Form 17M Multifamily Annual Certification Report, within 90 days after its fiscal year-end. Form 17M includes a general information statement, and audited financial statements and report on internal control structure.
- Once approved, each Servicer-only counterparty must submit Form 1110M Multifamily Annual Certification Report annually to Freddie Mac.

Quarterly Reporting: In addition to the annual requirement set forth in the preceding paragraph, each approved institution is required to submit Form 1055, Mortgage Bankers Financial Reporting Form, after the end of each calendar quarter.

 

Application Package

As part of the Application, please include the following documentation for review.

  1. Resumes: Each applicant must submit resumes of the following individuals:
    • Principals (stockholders with holdings of 10 percent or more)
    • Managing Executives and Key Personnel, defined as Chief Executive Officer, Chief Operating Officer, Chief Financial Officer, Chief Servicing Officer, Chief Architect/Engineer, Chief Underwriter, Chief Investor Reporting Officer, Corporate Insurance Contact, Credit Risk Officer(s), Head of Servicing, Head of Production and/or the equivalent of each of these positions.
    • Key operating personnel (division/operating unit heads) in the following areas:
      • Mortgage Administration
      • Default management (delinquency and foreclosure)
      • REO servicing
      • Investor accounting

Each resume should include an employment history, with titles and a description of the individual’s responsibilities in each functional area (for example, origination) for each position, years of experience in each area, educational background, and related formal industry training. Please also email each individual's date of birth. This information will be used to conduct the background check.

  1. Financial Statements: Each applicant must submit an audited annual report (financial statement), including a detailed explanation if the audit opinion is other than unqualified, for its most recent fiscal year-end and audited financial statements for the preceding two fiscal years (total of three years) and financial statements from the past 5 quarters. Note: If the most recent audited annual report is more than six months old, a supplemental financial statement (no more than 90 days old) certified by the applicant’s chief financial officer is required. The financial statements submitted should specifically tie the applicant’s financial statements to the parent’s audited financial statements
  2. System and Organization Control Reports: Provide a copy of the most recent SOC1, SOC2, SAS 70 report, if applicable.
  3. Entity Organizational Chart: Provide an entity organizational chart showing the applicant's direct and ultimate parents.
  4. Business Strategy:
    • Provide the applicant's business plan.
    • Provide a description of the products offered.
  5. Business Continuity Plan: Provide the applicant's Business Continuity Plan.
  6. Organizational Charts: Provide detailed organizational chart(s) showing all Production, Underwriting, Asset Management & Loan Servicing, Construction Monitoring and Conversion Management functional areas and the names and titles of all related personnel.
  7. Policies & Procedures: Provide all internal written multifamily policies & procedures. These should include underwriting, origination and servicing policies and procedures. Freddie Mac Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) will review the relevant P&Ps to assess the adequacy of each applicable area.
    • See supplemental P&Ps document attached with application package.
  8. Miscellaneous Multifamily Loan Information: Samples of typical payment history reports, collection letters, inspection reports (for not previously approved Seller/Servicers), and any other pertinent information regarding multifamily loans.
  9. Corporate Insurance: Provide a copy of the declaration page of the binder or other acceptable written evidence of corporate insurance. The documentation should specify which entity is covered, type of coverage, coverage amounts, loss payee, deductible, and policy duration.
  10. Warehouse Line: If applicable, provide a copy of the applicant's warehouse line of compliance certification.
  11. Information Security: Provide documentation addressing the following items
    • Information security policies and procedures, including information handling and privacy policies
    • Controls to prevent, detect and resolve/minimize impact of IT/information security incidents
    • Documentation of any relevant review results
  12. Vendor Risk Management: Provide documentation addressing the following items
    • Please complete the supplemental Seller/Servicer Vendor Inventory
    • Vendor risk management policies and procedures
  13. Internal Control Questionnaire: Complete the supplemental internal control questionnaire (ICQ) that is attached with the application package.
  14. Loan Insurance: Provide the applicant's insurance requirements that are maintained for loans retained in the applicant's portfolio.
